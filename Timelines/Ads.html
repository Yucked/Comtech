Advertisement Timeline

History of Advertising Pre-modern era - 2016

  • Egyptians started using papyrus to make sale messages and wall posters that are similar to the billboards we currently use. They also had ads about lost and found objects. Wall painting and rock art were used in India as a method of advertisement that is dated back to 4000 BC. Ancient China used copper painting plates to show images to promote local shops and businesses.

  • The first newspaper advertisement was published in the Boston News-Letter where a buyer posted an announcement seeking for an estate at Oyster Bay, Long Island.

  • The first billboard was made to publish advertisement.

  • The first advertising agency started in Philadelphia, USA. It was started by Volney B.

  • Companies started to sell their brand as well as their products through advertising.

  • The first advertisement was being announced on Radio. The American government also started to promote advertisement.

  • The Secretary of Commerce, Herbert Hoover gave a speech called "Advertising is a vital force in our National Life" to address the Associated Advertising Clubs of the World.

  • The first television commercial was shown.

  • Focus group and research were being used for the advancement of advertising.

  • The first infomercials come out on television.

  • First banner ad was put out by AT&T.

  • Yahoo launches search ads where people can search with keywords or phrases of their desired topic.

  • Google introduces AdWords where advertisers pay to publish brief advertisements of their service offerings, product listings, video content, and other services within the Google ad network to the web users.

  • Google Analytics was released which is part of the Google Marketing Platform that tracks and reports website traffic.

  • Facebook also started to launch advertisements.

  • Facebook allows demographic targeting where advertisers focus their online advertisements at consumers based on the demographic information they provided when they signed up on Facebook.

  • Google started interest-based advertising on its partner sites and YouTube which allows advertisers to reach consumers based on their interests and demographics.

  • Twitter starts promotional trends and tweets where people post their follow-up with the latest trends.

  • Michael Kors posted the first Instagram Ads.

  • Snapchat started to post online Advertisements.
